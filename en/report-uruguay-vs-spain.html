📋 Quick Summary (Read This First)

This is a clash of two World Cup winners in the Group H final round (and the only "former-champions duel" of the group stage), played simultaneously with Cape Verde-Saudi Arabia. Spain on 4 points (0-0 vs Cape Verde + a 4-0 thrashing of Saudi Arabia, GD +4, two clean sheets) need only a draw to lock up top spot in the group (in theory only "Cape Verde beating Saudi Arabia by 5 goals or more in the parallel match" could overhaul them on goal difference — a remote chance), and a win removes all doubt; Uruguay on 2 points (1-1 Saudi Arabia + 2-2 Cape Verde, GD 0) must win to secure progress (a win, provided Cape Verde do not beat Saudi Arabia, would even take top spot), with a draw leaving only a slim third-place tail decided on goal difference. That places the full "must-win intensity" on Uruguay alone — they have to commit forward, and that plays straight into the hands of an already-qualified, relaxed Spain set up to counter-attack and grind out possession. The market and the supercomputers cast Spain as heavy favourites: Spain win roughly 1.50–1.57, draw 3.40–4.40, Uruguay win 5.50–7.52 (Opta supercomputer 62.2% / 22.1% / 15.8%, Sofascore 58/24/18); the handicap line is Spain -1 / -1.5, and the total line O/U 2.5 is near pick'em (leaning slightly Under, BTTS leaning No). The biggest uncertainty is both sides missing key players: Spain's midfield hub Pedri is suspended after two rounds of accumulated yellow cards (multiple sources confirm), forcing De la Fuente to reshape the midfield; Uruguay are down to a double injury blow — defensive lynchpin Ronald Araújo (calf) and attacking midfielder De Arrascaeta (calf) are both out (multiple sources confirm), and Bielsa may gamble by pushing Darwin Núñez up as the No. 9. Three things to watch: ① Uruguay must win vs Spain's clean sheets + counter-attacking trap (the motivation gap); ② how Spain rebuild their midfield after Pedri's suspension (is Rodri as a lone holder solid enough); ③ can Núñez/Valverde break down Spain's zero-conceded back line. Base-case script: with possession, Spain control the win or grind out a draw to top the group, market heat index ≈ 3/5 (consensus is strong, one-sided toward Spain, with the information concentrated in handicap depth and Under/BTTS).

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what each of these "xG-family" metrics means)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; stripping out penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you; measures real defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = lots of long-range efforts / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
