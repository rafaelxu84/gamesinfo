📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Czechia 0-3 Mexico (HT 0-0) · 06-24

① Score progression

A clean sheet in the first half, with all the drama held over to the second: 39-year-old goalkeeper Ochoa made a record sixth World Cup appearance, and the two sides cancelled each other out at the break. In the 55th minute Romo threaded a pass and Mateo Chávez slotted into the far corner to break the deadlock; in the 61st minute Julián Quiñones netted his second goal of the tournament to double the lead; in stoppage time Alvarado set it up and Álvaro Fidalgo sealed 3-0 in the 90+ minute. Mexico won all three matches, finished on 9 points to top Group A, and became the first Mexico side ever to win all their group-stage matches; Czechia finished bottom (1 point) and were eliminated.

③ Tactical review

Winning on efficiency, not by storming — but that is exactly Mexico's repeatable model this tournament
Just 54% possession and actually fewer shots than Czechia (11 vs 13), yet Mexico scored 3 from 5 on-target efforts. This shows this Mexico side has settled into a stable approach of "patient control + high-quality finishing", winning all three not on spectacle but on taking their chances — heading into the knockouts, that is a more sustainable way to win.
Stepping on the gas only in the second half = a sign of fitness and bench depth
All three goals came after the 55th minute, with two substitute/rotation players (Chávez, Fidalgo) involved. This shows Aguirre's rotation and second-half tempo lift were deliberate, and Mexico can wear opponents down late in the game — especially lethal against minnows running on empty.
Czechia's "quantity over quality" exposed a missing finisher
13 shots, only 1 on target, 0.47 xG. This shows Czechia lack a finisher who can turn chances into threats, which is the core reason they finished bottom and went out — long-range, hopeful shooting is almost useless against a high-quality defensive line.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what each of these "xG-like" metrics means)

xG / npxG: total quality of shot chances; excluding penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opponent creates against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = lots of long-range/poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
