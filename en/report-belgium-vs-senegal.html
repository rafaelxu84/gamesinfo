📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full-time Belgium 3-2 Senegal (AET · 2-2 reg) · 07-01

① Score Progression

Seattle, Lumen Field: one of the epic comebacks in World Cup knockout history. Senegal at one point held a two-goal lead—Habib Diarra and Ismaïla Sarr scored in turn to put Belgium 0-2 down, with only about 5 minutes of regulation left. In that desperate moment, substitute Romelu Lukaku scored in the 86th minute to pull one back, and just 3 minutes later captain Youri Tielemans headed in a Leandro Trossard cross to make it 2-2 in the 89th minute, forcing extra time. Extra time stayed deadlocked until the final moment, when Tielemans coolly slotted home a penalty in the 125th minute (official time 124:44) to seal it 3-2—considered the latest winning goal in World Cup history. Belgium advance to the Round of 16 with a stunning comeback and will face the USA; Senegal, sitting on 3.54 xG for the match (far above Belgium's 1.80), threw away a strong position and go out at the Round of 32.

③ Tactical Review

The pre-match warning "Belgium's attack is shaky, don't underestimate a draw/extra time" landed precisely
The pre-match snapshot clearly noted Belgium had misfired in the first two rounds with a shaky attack, and that low-scoring and draw/extra-time probabilities were not low (Belgium no-vig ≈46% / draw 30%). The result: Belgium went 0-2 down, didn't equalize until the 89th minute, forced extra time and edged it only via a 125th-minute penalty. Transferable takeaway: Belgium's attacking output is extremely unstable and tends to get bogged down against high-pressing opponents; their progression relied on leaders delivering in the moment rather than systemic stability—this is the biggest uncertainty against the USA.
Senegal's 3.54 xG + 2 missed big chances = a textbook case of "playing a great hand badly"
Pre-match we singled out Sarr/Mané's transition pace as the key tool to wear Belgium down and create counters—the result: Senegal went 2-0 up precisely on Diarra's and Sarr's efficient raids, with a match xG of 3.54 completely suppressing Belgium. Transferable takeaway: Senegal have the chance-creation to beat any strong side, but their "kill the game" efficiency and mental management are a fatal weakness—unable to add another after 2-0, missing 2 big chances, they were ultimately overturned.
Belgium's aging defense was cut open twice by Sarr/Mané's transition pace
Pre-match we warned that Belgium's aging defense + Mendy's absence left soft spots at both ends, and flagged "if Belgium over-commit, beware Sarr/Mané in-behind transitions." The result: Belgium conceded twice on exactly Senegal's two fast transitions. Transferable takeaway: Belgium's back line is extremely fragile against balls in behind for pacey wingers—the USA's Pulisic/Weah/Tillman wide pace will directly replicate this soft spot.
Super-sub Lukaku + Tielemans's leadership are Belgium's hidden depth
Garcia bringing on Lukaku immediately changed the comeback's tempo, while Tielemans scored a brace with a header + a penalty. Transferable takeaway: Belgium's bench and leadership quality can bail them out when behind, but they over-rely on "individual moments" rather than a system—if those players are shut down, Belgium's attacking ceiling collapses quickly.
