① Score progression

Seattle's "quasi-knockout": Bosnia stormed out of the blocks with 5 shots in the first 23 minutes (all from outside the box). In the 29th minute, 18-year-old prodigy Kerim Alajbegović drilled a 20-yard strike into the near corner from outside the area — becoming the 8th-youngest goalscorer in World Cup history; in the 34th minute Qatar goalkeeper Abunada turned the ball into his own net for 2-0; in the 42nd minute Al-Haydos pulled one back for Qatar to make it 2-1 at the break. In the 82nd minute, Ermin Mahmić (who had also scored in the 1-4 loss to Switzerland) added another to seal 3-1. Bosnia reach the World Cup knockout rounds for the first time in their history.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what each of these "xG-type" metrics represents)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; excluding penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — a low value = lots of long-range efforts / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial dominance rather than pure possession.
