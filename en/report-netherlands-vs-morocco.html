📊 Post-Match Review: Tactics & Data · Full time Netherlands 1-1 Morocco (pens 3-2, Morocco advance) · HT 0-0 · 06-29

① Scoreline & Flow

Estadio Monterrey: a "Netherlands pinned back, equalised at the death, eliminated on penalties" 1-1 (3-2 pens). Morocco controlled throughout (70% possession, xG 1.40 vs 0.23) but couldn't convert; the Netherlands switched to a back five and sat deep to counter. The deadlock broke in the 71st minute — substitute Wout Weghorst made an immediate impact, heading a long ball to send Summerville through, and the winger squared for Cody Gakpo to finish in the 72nd minute, Netherlands 1-0. Morocco refused to give up and equalised in stoppage time: Issa Diop's header in the 90+1' (official 90:05) glanced Talbi's in-swinging cross past Verbruggen, getting between Van Dijk and Koopmeiners to make it 1-1 and force extra time. Extra time produced just one shot — but the biggest chance of the match: Soufiane Rahimi went clean through, only for a stunning Verbruggen save. In the shootout Morocco won 3-2: Bounou saved Summerville's spot-kick, and Saibari scored the decisive penalty. Morocco advance to the Round of 16 (now winners of both their World Cup shootouts, the other being the 2022 R16 win over Spain) and face Canada in Houston on July 4; the Netherlands were knocked out on penalties at back-to-back World Cups, exiting at the Round of 32 (one of their earliest World Cup exits).

③ Tactical Review

Morocco turned the Netherlands into a defensive side via control + a high press — but nearly paid for wasted chances
The pre-match read had Morocco as a resilient disruptor with the Netherlands slightly favoured on individual quality. In reality Morocco flipped the script entirely: 70% possession, 102 final-third entries, 5 big chances, forcing the Netherlands into passive defending. Transferable conclusion: Morocco are not a park-the-bus minnow — they can dominate a traditional power through possession and pressing, but their finishing (1 goal from 5 big chances) is the bottleneck holding them back from going further.
The Netherlands' in-game switch to a back five chose security but sacrificed creativity
Koeman went to a back five from the start for the first time since March 2024 (four defenders in the 31 games between), aiming to contain Hakimi and Morocco's wing thrust. Defensively it held for long stretches, but the attack shrivelled — 6 shots and 0.23 xG across 120 minutes, the only goal coming via substitute Weghorst's flick-on and Gakpo's cool finish. Transferable conclusion: the Netherlands' conservative reshape contained the opponent but locked themselves in too — when individual quality can't be cashed in open play, a deep back five is a double-edged sword for this side.
Set-pieces and substitutes were the Netherlands' only route to a goal — not enough over 120'
The Dutch goal came from substitute Weghorst's aerial flick (long ball duel → Summerville → Gakpo), and Morocco's equaliser from Talbi's in-swinging cross and Diop's header. Transferable conclusion: both goals came from second balls / crosses rather than build-up play, confirming that in knockouts set-pieces and aerial threat are often the decisive variable for breaking a packed or deep block; beyond an impact substitute, the Netherlands lacked a sustainable open-play route.
The shootout was a triumph of chaos and goalkeeping — Morocco's penalty DNA delivers again
The shootout saw woodwork and misses on both sides (El Aynaoui hit the crossbar, Kluivert the post, Timber fired wide, Hakimi the left post), before Bounou saved Summerville and Saibari scored the winner. Transferable conclusion: Morocco have now won both their World Cup shootouts (Spain 2022, Netherlands here), with Bounou the embodiment of their penalty edge; the Netherlands, eliminated on penalties at back-to-back World Cups, now carry penalties as a major-tournament demon.
