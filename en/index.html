In other words, "backing the strong team on the handicap" came up empty in nearly half the matches — Switzerland held by Qatar, Türkiye losing to Australia, Ecuador beaten by Côte d'Ivoire's last-gasp winner, the Netherlands held twice (Japan, Sweden) are all textbook cases of the underdog covering.

✅ Strong scripts that delivered: the genuine "win and cover" results mostly came from the elite giants unleashing their firepower — Argentina 3-0 Algeria (Messi hat-trick to top the World Cup scoring charts), France's two straight wins (Mbappé equaling Klose on 16), Norway's Haaland scoring twice in back-to-back games, Spain 4-0 Saudi Arabia, Germany's two 7-1s. In these matches the handicap + over largely landed — the one side where favorite pricing held up.

• Canada 1-1 Bosnia: Facundo Tello (Argentina) showed just 1 yellow all game and let the physical duels run, the polar opposite of Sampaio.
• South Korea 2-1 Czechia: Egypt's Amin Mohamed refereed his first World Cup match, with steady game control.
