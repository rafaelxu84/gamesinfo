Referee Watch · Reading the officiating so far (through 6/14)

The referees' thresholds across the opening matches span a huge range, sharply amplifying how much "who's whistling" matters for card markets:

• Opener (Mexico 2-0 South Africa): Wilton Sampaio (Brazil, career 5.08 yellows/game, one of the strictest worldwide) showed 3 reds, a World Cup opener record — but that fits his zero-tolerance + VAR-backed profile, so it's no surprise; the signal is that FIFA is willing to put a strict whistle on the marquee game and set a strict tone from the start.
• USA 4-1 Paraguay: Danny Makkelie (Netherlands) used VAR to overturn a wrong yellow on Ream and to penalize Almirón for a dive — a textbook correction, putting "dives will be punished" on record.
• Canada 1-1 Bosnia: Facundo Tello (Argentina) showed just 1 yellow all game and let the physical duels run, the polar opposite of Sampaio.
• South Korea 2-1 Czechia: Egypt's Amin Mohamed refereed his first World Cup match, with steady game control.
