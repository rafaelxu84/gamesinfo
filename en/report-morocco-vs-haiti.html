① Score progression

A goal feast in Atlanta: Haiti scored at a World Cup for the first time in 52 years (the 10th minute, also Haiti's first goal of this tournament) to lead briefly, before the two sides traded blows. Achraf Hakimi (39'), Ismael Saibari (45+1') equalised/put Morocco ahead; Haiti's Wilson Isidor (43') struck again, making it a 2-2 battle at the break. Morocco advance to the round of 32 (2nd in Group C); Haiti are eliminated after two games but still scored 2 goals here — beaten but unbowed. ③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: total quality of shot chances; with penalties stripped out it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring the true level of the defence.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — low value = lots of long shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than pure possession.
