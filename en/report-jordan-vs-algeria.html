📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Algeria 2-1 Jordan (HT 0-1) · 06-22

① Score progression

The Santa Clara night session delivered a script reversal: debutants Jordan struck first — in the 36th minute Mousa Al-Tamari drove into the box on the right and cut the ball back, an Algeria defender's clearance was mistimed, and Nizar Al-Rashdan followed up to fire a low first-time shot past Luca Zidane's fingertips; Jordan 1-0, taking the lead into the dressing room at the break. Algeria adjusted in the second half: half-time substitute Benbouali headed home from a corner to equalise (goalkeeper Abulaila misjudged it, diving the wrong way); then in the 82nd minute another corner — also a substitute, Amine Gouiri, poked home in a goalmouth scramble, completing the Algeria 2-1 turnaround and keeping their qualification hopes alive. Jordan, beaten in both matches, are out; Algeria's win at the same time confirmed Argentina as Group J winners ahead of schedule.

③ Tactical review

Algeria's "finishing failure" was not fixed — it was bypassed via dead balls
Both goals came from corners, and both were scored by substitutes — open play still failed to crack the packed defence (the same root cause as the 0 shots on target in MD1 vs Argentina). This shows Algeria's open-play finishing remains a structural Achilles' heel; this win came more from set pieces and the manager's substitutions than from a repair of the attacking system — facing a stronger Austria in the final round, if the dead balls misfire and open play still jams up, scoring will once again be a problem.
Jordan's set-piece soft spot was confirmed to the letter
The pre-flagged "corner-defence vulnerability" (the MD1 own goal came from a corner) was again fatal here — both goals conceded came from corners, and the goalkeeper made a misjudgment too. This shows Jordan's set-piece defending is a systemic shortcoming, not a one-off: height/marking/keeper-coming-out decisions need a systematic fix, otherwise they will keep bleeding against any opponent with aerial presence.
Jordan's open-play content was again above expectations; they lost on raw quality and bench depth
Jordan again scored first, and the quality of Al-Tamari's drive and cut-back was very high; in open play they were not outplayed. This shows this debutant side's floor is not low and their counter-attacking patterns are taking shape — what truly opened the gap was the Europe-based quality of Algeria's bench (Gouiri/Benbouali directly changed the game), a depth gap Jordan cannot currently bridge.
Substitutions decided the match — Algeria's manager's in-game adjustments were the deciding factor
Two players coming on at half-time/in the second half accounted for both goals. This shows Algeria's real edge is in squad depth rather than the starting system: when the starters' open play jammed up, the individual quality off the bench became the key to breaking through — also a card they can replicate against Austria in the final round.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: total quality of shot chances; with penalties excluded, it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — Algeria had 0 shots on target in MD1, a broken finishing chain.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
