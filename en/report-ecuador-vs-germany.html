📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Ecuador 2-1 Germany (HT 1-1) · 06-25

① Score progression

At MetLife in New Jersey, Ecuador staged a comeback to topple an already-qualified Germany and pull off a shock to reach the last 32. Germany struck early — Leroy Sané in the 2nd minute (109 seconds) opened the scoring (Ecuador protested a high foot by Pavlović on Vite in the build-up, but the goal stood after a VAR check); yet Ecuador responded just 7 minutes later, Nilson Angulo in the 9th minute curling a long-range strike from outside the box to level — Ecuador's first goal of the tournament (the duck was finally broken on the team's 40th attempt). In the second half Germany were awarded a penalty (Ordóñez foul on Havertz), but referee Tori Penso, after a pitch-side VAR review (ruling Sané had earlier fouled Vite), overturned the penalty; then Gonzalo Plata in the 77th minute poked home from a corner — flicked on by Rodríguez's header — getting there ahead of a misjudged Neuer charge to seal the win. Germany lost but, on goal difference of +7 (6 pts), still won Group E, with Ivory Coast second and Curaçao eliminated; Ecuador climbed to 4 pts and went through as one of the best third-placed teams — their first World Cup knockout-stage appearance since 2006.

③ Tactical review

Germany's "strongest XI + zero rotation" still got turned over — this exposes a settled-build-up problem, not rotation
The biggest pre-match variable, "Germany rotation", actually landed as zero rotation, the strongest XI (Neuer + Sané/Musiala/Wirtz/Havertz), with Sané striking inside 2 minutes, yet the full-match xG was just 0.65. It shows that even fielding their full first-choice team, Germany still could not consistently prise open a disciplined low block — this is a structural settled-build-up problem, not fatigue or experimentation, and a genuine warning sign for their knockout-stage prospects.
Ecuador broke their goal duck and immediately turned the game — the "few chances, high quality" model worked
Just 7 shots all match (fewest in a single game this tournament) yielded 1.51 xG and 2 goals. It shows Beccacece's system relies not on possession or shot volume, but on converting scarce chances into high-quality shots — Angulo's long-range strike and Plata's set-piece scramble were precisely the only two realistic scoring routes named pre-match, and both delivered.
Switching to 4-4-2 was the structural precondition for the comeback
Ecuador abandoned the pre-match-predicted 3-5-2 wing-back system and started officially in 4-4-2 with two strikers, bringing Angulo into the XI to add wide threat and long-range directness (the 9' strike came from this more attacking structure). It shows that, in a must-win situation, Beccacece actively doubled down on attacking risk rather than purely sitting deep — a tactical choice to "play for the win, not the draw".
The VAR-overturned Germany penalty was a match-deciding in-game swing
Early in the second half Germany were awarded a penalty, then it was overturned by Penso after a pitch-side review (ruling Sané had earlier fouled Vite). It shows the balance of this match tilted on one key decision — had the penalty stood, Germany would most likely have retaken the lead and the comeback script might have been rewritten; Penso's willingness to both whistle and reverse matches her pre-match profile.

📋 Quick Summary (Read This First)

This is the Group E final round's "asymmetric motivation" clash: Germany on 6 points (7-1 thrashing of Curaçao + 2-1 comeback over Ivory Coast, GD +7) have already secured passage to the last 32, and their only target here is to lock up the group top spot and seed status, with manager Nagelsmann having publicly stated the lineup will "probably be a mix" — meaning heavy rotation is the biggest variable of this match; Ecuador on 1 point (0-1 loss to Ivory Coast + 0-0 with Curaçao, GD -1, 0 goals across two rounds) have been pushed to the brink — they must beat Germany to reach 4 points to have a chance of catching the last 32 via one of the "best third place" spots (48-team format: top two in each group qualify directly + 8 best third-placed teams fill out the bracket), and a draw or defeat essentially ends them. The biggest uncertainty is the scale of Germany's rotation and their mindset: already through + centre-back Schlotterbeck confirmed out for the rest of the tournament with an ankle injury (a forced change, Rüdiger replaces him), Nagelsmann may rest core players such as Wirtz/Musiala and hand starts to backups like Undav; Ecuador, meanwhile, are fighting for survival with maximum intensity, and have been defensively solid this tournament (just 1 goal conceded in two rounds, holding Curaçao's keeper Eloy Room to 0-0 despite 15 saves). Three things to watch: ① Germany's rotation depth and its impact on their control and ceiling for breaking down a packed defence; ② whether Ecuador's low block + counter-attacks/set pieces can seize a slim chance despite their goal duck; ③ the motivation gap — Germany conserving energy vs Ecuador going all out. ③ Deep metrics quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shot chances; excluding penalties it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — a low value = many long-range shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial dominance rather than mere possession.
