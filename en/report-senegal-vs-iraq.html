📋 Quick Summary (Read This First)

This is a Group I final-round "end of the road and the bottom" clash between two sides on 0 points, played simultaneously with Norway-France. France and Norway, both on 6 points, have locked up the top two and qualified together — this match is meaningless for the top two; all that remains to be contested is "the faint tail of third-place qualification + avoiding the bottom + ranking/morale". Key correction: the earlier media claim that "both teams are already eliminated" is inaccurate — under FIFA's 48-team format, the best 8 of the 12 third-placed teams advance to the round of 32, so both teams are not yet mathematically eliminated; but Senegal's goal difference is −3 and Iraq's is −6, so only Senegal, via a heavy win, retains an extremely slim third-place chance, while Iraq is all but hopeless (even a win can hardly fill a −6 hole). This pushes the nature of the match toward "Senegal must win big, Iraq plays for pride". The odds are completely one-sided: Senegal to win around 1.27 (American −375), Draw 4.50 (+350), Iraq to win 10.00 (+900), de-vigged and normalised to roughly 71% / 20% / 9%; the handicap projects Senegal as a heavy favourite (around −1.5 / −2), and the totals line O/U mostly sits at 2.5. The biggest uncertainties: ① Senegal goalkeeper Mendy confirmed out with a knee injury, replaced by Mory Diaw; ② Iraq captain and top scorer Aymen Hussein (limped off in the 26th minute against France) very likely absent, with Mohanad Ali also expected to miss out, leaving the line led by Ali Al-Hamadi. Three things to watch: ① can Senegal produce a "heavy win" (just 2.25 total xG in their first two games — efficiency is the soft spot); ② how much attack does Iraq have left without its key striker; ③ motivation mismatch — Senegal must take risks chasing more goals, Iraq sit deep for pride and counter. ③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; excluding penalties, it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you; measures true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = lots of long shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than mere possession.
