📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Croatia 2-1 Ghana (HT 1-0) · Lincoln Financial · 06-27

① How the Score Unfolded

The "must-win vs through-with-a-draw" script delivered the high intensity expected from the motivation mismatch: Croatia went ahead through Petar Sučić's stunner from outside the box in the 31st minute, leading 1-0 at the break. After halftime Ghana levelled through Derrick Luckassen in the 73rd minute from a set piece, briefly tilting things their way; but Croatia won it in the 83rd minute as Nikola Vlašić headed home — assisted by 40-year-old Luka Modrić from a corner, making Modrić the oldest player on record (since 1966) to provide a World Cup assist. Croatia won 2-1 and qualify as Group L runners-up to the round of 32; Ghana, despite the defeat, still go through as one of the best third-placed teams (4 points), so both sides advance.

③ Tactical Review

"Croatia must win → forced to push up → space for Ghana's counter" — the motivation-gap script played out, but Croatia broke through on individual quality
The preview flagged "Croatia forced to chase the win, leaving space for Ghana's counter". In the event Ghana did sit deep and even levelled from a set piece; but Croatia broke through via Sučić's long-range shot + Vlašić's header (neither from open-play penetration). Transferable conclusion: against a low block, Croatia's stable scoring outlets are long shots and set pieces (Modrić's dead-ball quality), not positional combinations — whether this "moment-of-magic output" repeats against stronger knockout sides is the key variable.
Ghana's "clean-sheet fortress" was breached — set pieces both saved and sank them
Ghana kept clean sheets in their first two games (0-0, 0-0); here they were breached by two non-open-play goals (a long shot + a corner header), while levelling via a set piece themselves. Transferable conclusion: Ghana's low block still works against open play (conceding just 0.57 xG), but defending set pieces and long shots is the weak spot; for the R32 vs Colombia they must shore up dead-ball defending, or they'll repeat being caught by a single decisive moment.
Modrić at 40 remains the ceiling for dead-ball and leadership value
The preview noted "Modrić looked his age vs Panama, but his experience and organisation are irreplaceable". Here he set up Vlašić's winner from a corner, becoming the oldest World Cup assist provider on record. Transferable conclusion: Modrić's open-play coverage and tempo will dip, but his set-piece quality and big-game reading remain a decisive Croatia weapon in the knockouts — building around his dead balls is an efficient route.
The xG inversion (Ghana 1.00 > Croatia 0.57) is a warning: Croatia won on efficiency, not process
Ghana's chance quality was actually higher; Croatia won with fewer, higher-quality finishes (4 on target, 2 goals). Transferable conclusion: Croatia's progress carries a genuine slice of luck, with the process data not in their favour; if they keep relying on "mediocre process + star magic" in the knockouts, the risk against more efficient strong sides (e.g. Portugal) is elevated.

If Ghana draw, they are all but certain to qualify regardless of the other result (even if they lose, it still depends on goal details, but going from GD 0 to a negative figure would most likely still beat Croatia's catch-up on three points — the final arithmetic here is TBC, but the mainstream analysis is unanimous: a draw is enough to go through). For Croatia to qualify, the safest route is to win; a draw could leave them level on 4 points with GD 0 but behind Ghana on goal difference (Ghana's two 0-0s give them GD 0; Croatia are currently GD -1 and would need extra goals to close the goal-difference gap), a complex situation dependent on the England-vs-Panama result. So Croatia are forced to chase the win, which creates genuine open space for attacking and at the same time gives Ghana's counter-attacking tactics ideal conditions. xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you; measures real defensive level. The attacking xG Ghana have conceded this tournament is TBC, but England's 19 shots for 1 on target is the most intuitive proxy.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength. Croatia's differential against England was catastrophic; Ghana's two games look strong-defence, weak-attack.
Big Chance: high-probability scoring chances — Croatia's xG 1.66 from just 6 shots vs Panama is average chance quality; Ghana created almost no big chances but a defensive breakdown into a counter can still trigger one.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press. Ghana sit deep, so their PPDA is high (allowing Croatia to pass), but the resilience of their organised defensive line is their real strength.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than raw possession. Croatia's tilt will be high when forced to attack, but that does not mean an effective threat — quality, not quantity, is key.
