📋 Quick Summary (Read This First)

This is the Group I final-round "top-spot decider, but both sides are already through" clash, and one of the talking points of this World Cup — Mbappé vs Haaland (both already on 4 goals, vying for the Golden Boot alongside Messi). The arithmetic is clear: France 6 pts, goal difference +5 (6 scored); Norway 6 pts, goal difference +4 (7 scored). Both sides beat the same two opponents in their opening Group I games (Senegal, Iraq); the only difference is France's one extra goal of difference — so France only need a draw to lock up top spot, while Norway must win to leapfrog them (when level on points the head-to-head comes first, and this match is the direct meeting, so the winner ranks higher). The real significance of top spot: the group winner faces one of the better third-placed teams in the round of 32 (seen as a relatively favourable draw, possibly Sweden, with a potential round-of-16 opponent on Germany's side); the runner-up most likely meets the Group E runner-up (Côte d'Ivoire / Ecuador) and could later run into Brazil / Netherlands, with England's side looming in the quarters — multiple outlets consider the top-spot draw more favourable. But both are already qualified + the turnaround is short, so both have openly said they will rotate: Norway boss Solbakken has publicly said "there will be some changes" (several players cramped up late against Senegal, the game ended with 9 men); France's Deschamps has returned home to France following the death of his mother, with assistant Guy Stéphan taking charge, and the squad depth allows midfield rotation. The model and the market both make France clear favourites: Opta's supercomputer has France 55.4% win / draw 21.6% / Norway 23.0%; the market has a France win around 1.62 (FanDuel -160), draw 4.50, Norway win 4.50 (+360–+400); on the Asian line France -1 (about 2.05) / Norway +1 (about 1.80), with the total leaning Over on O/U 2.5 (Over around -150). Three things to watch: ① how heavily each side rotates (decides intensity and upset probability); ② whether the Haaland/Mbappé Golden Boot race keeps both attacking at full tilt (pushing the Over); ③ the motivation gap between Norway needing to win and France being satisfied with a draw. Base-case script: goals, with France either edging it or drawing to secure top spot, overheat index ≈ 2/5 (consensus leans France but the price has not been driven by hype, with the disagreement over rotation and over/under).

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-style" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; with penalties removed it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
Big chance: a high-probability scoring chance — Norway have 10 at this tournament, the most in Group I.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
