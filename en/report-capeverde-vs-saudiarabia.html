📋 Quick Summary (read this first)

This is a Group H final-round win-or-go-home clash where a victory likely sends either side through, and it is played simultaneously with Uruguay-Spain, with the two results feeding into each other. Standings before the final round: Spain lead on 4 pts (GD +4) and have all but locked top spot; Uruguay and Cape Verde are level on 2 pts (both GD 0); Saudi Arabia are bottom on 1 pt (GD -4). Cape Verde (at their first ever World Cup) advance into the top two and mathematically clinch progression with a win; a draw means they need Spain to beat Uruguay to stay in the top two, otherwise they finish third and depend on the "eight best third-placed teams" rule and other groups' results (2 pts is usually not enough); a loss essentially eliminates them. Saudi Arabia, by contrast, must win — a win takes them to 4 pts, and "4 points is usually enough to advance as a best third-placed team", while if Spain also beat Uruguay, Saudi Arabia could even go straight into the top two; any non-win (draw or loss) essentially eliminates them. That gives both teams a strong incentive to go for the win, but Saudi Arabia have their backs to the wall more (they must win, and a -4 goal difference is a heavy burden). The market makes Cape Verde slight favorites: Cape Verde win approx. 2.35, draw 3.50, Saudi Arabia win 2.90 (vig-removed implied probabilities ≈ 40% / 27% / 33%); the Kalshi prediction market is around 38% / 29% / 35% — a near three-way even matchup. The total line is O/U 2.5 leaning Under (Under @-127): both sides are pragmatic and Cape Verde are defensively resilient (just 1 loss in their last 17 over 90 minutes). The biggest certainty variable is Cape Verde defender Sidny Lopes being suspended on a second yellow (multi-source confirmed), plus Telmo Arcanjo (hamstring) and Jovane Cabral (knock) TBC; on the Saudi side the squad is fully healthy with no suspensions, with captain Salem Al-Dawsari and forward Firas Al-Buraikan both available. Three things to watch: ① qualification arithmetic + the simultaneous result feeding in; ② Cape Verde's defensive resilience vs Saudi Arabia's counterattack; ③ the openness from both teams needing to win vs the pragmatic, Under-leaning baseline. Base scenario: low-scoring, a narrow Cape Verde win or a draw, market-heat index ≈ 2/5 (consensus mildly favors Cape Verde, no one-sided frenzy).

③ Deep-metric glossary (what these "xG-style" metrics each mean)

xG / npxG: total quality of shooting chances; excluding penalties, npxG better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — low value = lots of long-range shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than mere possession.
