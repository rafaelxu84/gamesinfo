📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Türkiye 3-2 USA (HT 2-1) · 06-25

① Score progression

SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles — a "dead rubber" final round whose script flipped completely: the USA, heavily rotated and already top of the group with no winning incentive; Türkiye going all out for redemption, finally scoring their first goal and recording their first win of the tournament in a match of pride. The USA struck early — Auston Trusty headed in on the 3rd minute (Sebastian Berhalter corner assist, two shots inside a single minute); but Türkiye replied quickly, Arda Güler levelling on the 10th minute (their first goal of the tournament) and Orkun Kökçü putting them ahead on the 31st minute, 2-1 at the break. In the second half Berhalter headed in to make it 2-2 on the 49th minute; the deadlock lasted into stoppage time, when substitute Kaan Ayhan scored a winner from close range on 90+8 for a 3-2 Türkiye victory. Key qualification conclusion: despite the defeat the USA still progress to the Round of 32 as Group D winners (goal difference / head-to-head keep them top); Türkiye go out with dignity — having scored 0 goals in their first two matches, they scored 3 in this one.

③ Tactical review

The motivation inversion was fully realised — in a dead rubber, "rotation vs full effort" was the real decider here
The USA rotated heavily (Turner started instead of Freese, most regulars rested, Pulisic not on until the 58th minute), with low winning intensity; Türkiye, though also rotated (Calhanoglu/Demiral/Kadioglu/Akturkoglu on the bench), went all out for redemption. This shows that in a final round with nothing at stake, the motivation gap can directly cancel out or even reverse the paper-quality gap — the pre-match "glued-together" pricing of "USA win ≈49%, draw / Türkiye steal a result probability raised" was validated as the correct direction, and tilted toward the Türkiye side.
The "no centre-forward" ailment persists, but the creative midfield finally converted chances into goals
Türkiye's 3 goals came from forward Güler, midfielder Kökçü and holding midfielder Kaan Ayhan respectively — none from a genuine No. 9. This shows the pre-match structural read of "lacking a genuine centre-forward" has not gone away, but the creativity of Montella's system (Güler's linking, set pieces and second-ball arrivals) was enough, against a rotated USA back line, to have midfield/wide finishers fill in and score — the 0 goals in the first two matches were more "poor luck + strong opposition" than a creativity collapse.
The stability of the USA's reserve back line dropped, breached twice — on a set piece and in stoppage-time management
The goals conceded came from an open-play go-ahead (Kökçü 31') and a stoppage-time winner (Ayhan on as a sub 88', scored 90+8'). This shows the rotated back line of Trusty/McKenzie/M.Robinson/Scally was clearly weaker than the first-choice line in understanding and game management (especially marking and second-ball protection in stoppage time) — exactly the pre-match call that "regulars rested → drop in back-line quality is a window for Türkiye to steal a result".
Pulisic's arrival raised the attacking ceiling, but could not cover for the rotated side defensively
Pulisic came on as a substitute on the 58th minute and found his rhythm (the knockout-stage warm-up purpose achieved), after which the USA held 2-2 and held the possession edge. This shows Pulisic is unmistakably the switch for the USA attack, but in an overall-rotated reserve side with discounted defending and game management, a single-point attacking upgrade is not enough to avoid conceding in stoppage time — in the knockouts the USA need their first-choice unit fully in place, not to be rescued by individual stars.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the sum of shot-chance quality; excluding penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the chance quality opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = many long-range efforts/poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than mere possession.
