📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time South Africa 1-0 South Korea (HT 0-0) · 06-24

① Score progression

One of the tournament's biggest upsets: South Korea boss Hong Myung-bo left captain Son Heung-min on the bench (Son's first non-start after 12 consecutive World Cup starts), and the first half held firm against South Africa. In the 63rd minute Moremi laid it off and Thapelo Maseko drilled in a long-range finish — the goal that took South Africa into the World Cup knockout rounds for the first time in their history. Son came on for the second half but could not turn it around. South Africa finished 2nd in Group A on 4 points; South Korea finished 3rd on 3 points and need to wait on the best-third-place ranking to qualify.

③ Tactical review

A textbook ultra-low-possession counter — South Africa willingly gave up the ball
31.5% possession (their lowest ever), yet they won and posted the higher xG. It shows South Africa knew their positional play was inferior to the opposition, so they simply sat deep and broke at speed through Maseko — the most pragmatic template for an underdog facing a strong possession side, and one they can repeat in the knockouts.
Hong Myung-bo's gamble of dropping Son backfired completely
South Korea benched their captain and only brought him on at half time; 68.5% possession all match but just 1.0 xG and 0 goals. It shows that without Son's finishing and link play, their possession was nothing more than "harmless sideways passing" — this rotation/conservative call directly surrendered the initiative on qualification, the biggest human variable of the night.
Maseko was South Africa's matchwinner
5 shots, 0.32 xG, and the only goal. It shows South Africa's attack leans heavily on Maseko's individual explosiveness — both a strength (a clear go-to spark) and a risk (if he is man-marked in the knockouts, the counter threat drops sharply).

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-style" metrics each mean)

xG / npxG: the total quality of chances; with penalties removed it better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opposition creates against you, a measure of true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality per shot — a low value = lots of long-range efforts / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial dominance rather than raw possession.
