📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time France 3-0 Iraq (HT 1-0) · 06-22

① Score progression

France cruised through and qualified early, in a match that also became this World Cup's first weather-suspended game. In the 14th minute Mbappé took a Michael Olise pass from the right and finished left-footed from outside the box for 1-0, which held to half-time. The second half restarted late after lightning + heavy rain forced a stoppage of more than two hours (the big screen advised spectators to seek shelter); after the resumption Mbappé pounced on a botched short-passing goal kick from Iraq — Dembélé intercepted the cross-field pass, Mbappé slotted home for his brace, his individual 16th World Cup goal, drawing level with Klose in second on the all-time list (behind only Messi); then Dembélé, set up by Olise, drilled a low shot into the bottom-left corner for 3-0. France, calm and clinical, comfortably exceeded their 2.38 xG and locked up their Group I qualification spot.

③ Tactical review

The Mbappé + Olise right-flank link-up = France's most efficient goal machine
Both the first and the third goal stemmed from Olise's work on the right, with Mbappé completing his brace. This shows that whenever Olise gets space to create on the right, France have a top-tier "pass-and-move + finishing" combination — Mbappé, level with Klose (16 goals), is in superb form and is France's biggest certainty heading into the knockouts. Shutting down that right-flank link-up will be the first task for any opponent trying to contain France.
The 2-hour weather stoppage did not unsettle France — it punished Iraq instead
The second half was interrupted by lightning for over two hours; after the restart France quickly extended the lead — including a second goal that came directly from a failed Iraqi short goal kick. This shows France's focus and immediate execution remained intact even after a lengthy stoppage, while Iraq committed a basic error on the restart and were punished, with the gap in psychology and experience magnified in unusual circumstances.
2.38 xG, 3 goals — France were truly dominant across the board
Unlike Argentina's economical "3 shots to win" mode, France generated 2.38 xG and converted cleanly. This shows France delivered a complete performance with both chance volume and efficiency on point, while Iraq posed 0 threat all game — the title-favourite billing was validated, and France can preserve form having already qualified early.
Iraq's attack produced nothing, and they fell apart mentally after the restart
Iraq created no real threat all game and scored 0, and even gifted a goal away through a goal-kick error after the restart. This shows that against a side of France's calibre, Iraq lack the means to turn defence into attack; in their final match they need to rediscover organisation and a finishing outlet against more evenly matched opposition.

③ Deep-metric glossary (what these "xG-style" metrics mean)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; excluding penalties reflects open-play creativity better.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opponent creates against you; measures true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — low value = lots of long shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than mere possession.
