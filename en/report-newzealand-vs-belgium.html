📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full-Time New Zealand 1-5 Belgium (HT 0-1) · 06-26

① How the goals came

Vancouver BC Place: a slow-starting Belgium fully cashed in their quality after the break, thrashing New Zealand 5-1 to take top spot in Group G and advance. Leandro Trossard broke the deadlock in the 28th minute (New Zealand failed to register a shot in the first half), then added a second in the 50th minute for his brace after the restart; Kevin De Bruyne made it 3-0 in the 66th minute. New Zealand pulled one back through Elijah Just in the 84th minute, but Belgium responded almost immediately — Romelu Lukaku in the 86th minute restored the three-goal cushion, and Alexis Saelemaekers in stoppage time (90+) added another to seal the 5-1. Belgium leapfrogged Egypt on goal difference to win Group G (Egypt drew 1-1 with Iran the same night); New Zealand finished bottom on 1 point and went out. This big win finally got De Bruyne, Lukaku and Trossard scoring in the same game, dispelling the misfiring gloom of "just 1 own goal" over the first two rounds.

③ Tactical Review

The "fancy build-up, no goals trap" was disproven: Belgium's value finally delivered
The biggest pre-match question was whether "Belgium, with just 1 own goal in two rounds, could finally fire in the final round", with a cautious "misfiring trap" warning attached. In the event Belgium won 5-1 with KDB + Lukaku + Trossard all scoring in the same game — an honest correction to the cautious read: when the opponent's level drops sharply and they must win, Belgium's individual quality is enough to be released all at once.
Trossard's brace + a multi-pronged wide/substitute attack were the breakthrough
Trossard, scoring once in each half, was the standout, and combined with De Bruyne's link-up and the finishes from Lukaku and Saelemaekers, Belgium's attack came from multiple sources — no longer reliant on a single focal point, a positive signal they can carry into the knockouts.
Ngoy's suspension did not break the defence, but the 84' goal exposed a concentration lapse
Pre-match the suspension of defensive anchor Ngoy and Garcia having to adjust at centre-back were flagged. Belgium's back line was untroubled for most of the game, but after going 3-0 up they conceded to Elijah Just in the 84th minute — a concentration lapse in garbage time, a small flaw to guard against in the knockouts.
New Zealand's "physical approach + will to win" could not overcome the quality gap; Wood's farewell ends in elimination
New Zealand had to win, yet failed to register a single shot in the first half, managing only Just's consolation goal over the match. The pre-match question of "can the physical approach spring an upset" was answered in the negative — the gap in strength and quality was amplified in the final round, and 34-year-old captain Chris Wood's (perhaps) last World Cup ended bottom of the group.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-type" metrics each mean)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; excluding penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — a low value = lots of long shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than mere possession.
