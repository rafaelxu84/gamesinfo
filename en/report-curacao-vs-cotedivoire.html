📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full-time Curaçao 0-2 Côte d'Ivoire (HT 0-1) · 06-25

① Scoring sequence

Philadelphia, Lincoln Financial Field: as expected pre-match, Côte d'Ivoire took control and methodically secured their qualification ticket. Nicolas Pépé scored a brace — in the 7th minute (actually 6:04) with a lightning strike assisted by Yan Diomande, the fastest goal in Côte d'Ivoire's World Cup history; and in the 64th minute off an Ibrahim Sangaré through-ball to double the lead. Pépé became the second Côte d'Ivoire player to score a World Cup brace in a single match since Aruna Dindane (vs Serbia) in 2006. Côte d'Ivoire recorded their second clean sheet of the tournament and advanced to the round of 32 as Group E runners-up (the nation's first-ever group-stage qualification, finally breaking their "group-stage curse" on a fourth appearance); Curaçao finished bottom on 1 point and were eliminated, their first-ever World Cup journey ending in the group stage. Germany, despite a 1-2 loss to Ecuador, still advanced as Group E winners.

③ Tactical review

The "controlled win" baseline script was executed precisely
63% possession, 1.30 vs 0.47 xG, both big chances converted. This shows that when holding the initiative for qualification, Côte d'Ivoire genuinely have the maturity to "manage" a match — not a frantic press, winning on chance quality rather than overwhelming volume (they actually had fewer shots than Curaçao). This was exactly the pre-match read of a "controlled win"; in the knockouts against stronger opponents, that pragmatism is a double-edged sword.
Pépé was the real match-winner not named pre-match
A brace, with the 7th-minute goal (6:04) the fastest in the nation's World Cup history. This shows that while the pre-match focus on Amad/Diomande's pace down the flanks did provide an assist (Diomande set up the first goal), the actual finishing point came from Pépé — Côte d'Ivoire's attacking threat is more distributed than expected, making it hard for knockout opponents to mark just one man.
The lightning start shattered Curaçao's low-block plan
Curaçao's only path pre-match was "deep low block + a heroic Room + counter-attack ambush", but they conceded in the 7th minute. This shows that once they fell behind and were forced to push up, Curaçao's structural shortcomings (a lack of transition depth and finishing points) were immediately exposed — 11 shots with only 2 on target and 0 big chances is the proof; goalkeeper Room could no longer rescue this one.
Curaçao's World Cup debut ended in "honour in defeat"
Three games, 1 point, bottom and eliminated, but as the lowest FIFA-ranked (#81 range) and least populous participating nation, the back-to-back clean sheet against Ecuador in the first two rounds was already a historic achievement. This shows the ceiling for this side is "stealing points with discipline and the goalkeeper" rather than consistently creating threat — the lack of a finishing edge is a long-term shortcoming to carry into future qualifying campaigns.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shot chances; excluding penalties better reflects open-play creation.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for composite strength.
xG per shot: quality per shot — a low value = many long-range shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value, the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than pure possession.
