📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time England 0-0 Ghana (HT 0-0) · Group L Matchday 2 · 06-23

① Score progression

Tuchel's England were held in Boston by a tightly organised Ghana, falling foul of the "second-match curse". England fired off 19 shots with possession as high as 78.8% (England's highest in a single World Cup match since records began in 1966, and the highest possession ever recorded by a team that failed to score at a World Cup), yet never broke open Ghana's low block. They did not register a shot on target until the second half, with Kane spurning a glorious late chance. Ghana managed just 2 shots all match (one of the lowest single-game totals of this tournament so far) yet held on for a point — a crucial draw for the Black Stars.

③ Tactical review

78.8% possession + 0 goals: a textbook case of England's "sterile possession"
England set the record for the highest possession by a team failing to score at a World Cup, yet managed only 14 box touches. Transferable conclusion: against a low block, England lacked the means to turn possession into quality chances inside the box (not enough half-space through-balls / vertical penetration), with the ball held mostly on the outside. This is the attacking malaise Tuchel must solve for the final match.
Ghana's low block + 2 shots: a victory for defensive discipline
Ghana managed just 2 shots all match yet held on for a point, with a disciplined back line and well-organised cover. Transferable conclusion: for mid-to-lower-tier sides against strong teams, "park the bus + occasional counter" remains an effective strategy, and possession-heavy sides like England need a dedicated plan for breaking down a packed defence.
Kane's late miss: a microcosm of England's "second-match curse"
England again ground to a stale draw in their second group match, with Kane spurning the best chance. Transferable conclusion: England's finishing is over-reliant on Kane, and when there is no second focal point they are very easy to neutralise.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; stripping out penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances opponents create against you, measuring the true level of your defence.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall strength.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = lots of long shots / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; lower = more aggressive pressing.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches, measuring territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
