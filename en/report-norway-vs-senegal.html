📊 Post-Match Review · Tactics & Data · Full time Norway 3-2 Senegal (HT 1-0) · 06-22

① Score progression

A rain-soaked MetLife staged the most nerve-jangling clash of the round, Norway edging it 3-2 to qualify alongside France. Just before half time, Marcus Holmgren Pedersen, on as a substitute, struck in the 43rd minute for 1-0; in the second half Haaland scored twice in quick succession — a close-range blast into the roof of the net in the 48th minute (becoming Norway's all-time World Cup top scorer); after Senegal's Sarr 53' pulled one back, Haaland latched onto an Ødegaard through-ball in the 58th minute and slotted past Mendy one-on-one to make it 3-1. Norway then dropped deep to defend and run down the clock, and Sarr's 90+3' goal kept the tie alive until the final whistle. Haaland scored 4 goals at this tournament so far, and in his 52nd cap notched his 58th and 59th international goals, becoming only the second player in nearly 50 years to "score a brace in each of his first two World Cup matches".

③ Tactical review

Norway = Haaland's team, Ødegaard is the can-opener
Of the 3 goals Haaland took 2, and the second came from an Ødegaard through-ball that put him one-on-one. This shows Norway's attacking model is crystal clear: Ødegaard hunts for through-balls in midfield, Haaland finishes up top — this "organiser + finisher" twin core is Norway's most repeatable scoring route, but it also means that if an opponent cuts off this line, Norway's attacking depth is limited (see the preview carry-over below).
Dropping deep after 3-1 nearly threw away a strong position
After going 3-1 up Norway visibly dropped deep and tried to run down the clock, only for Sarr to score again in stoppage time and reopen the contest. This shows Norway's game management while leading still has flaws — abandoning the press too early gave the opponent a window to fight back; this "can't hold a lead" tendency could prove fatal against stronger opposition in the knockouts.
Sarr is Senegal's spark, but the whole team over-relies on individual flashes
Both Senegal goals came from Sarr (2 goals from around 0.3 xG, finishing above his model). This shows Senegal have top-tier individual quality to stay in the score, but lack systematic, sustained creation — suppressed by Norway for most of the match, they only clawed back to 3-2 through Sarr's individual ability; attacking sustainability is insufficient.
Rain + tension = the most watchable open contest of the round
In the wet the two sides combined for 5 goals, with the outcome in doubt until the whistle — the most fiercely contested "six-pointer" of the round. This shows it was an evenly matched, end-to-end game — Norway won on Haaland's individual ceiling, not on overall dominance; had Haaland's form dipped slightly, the result could easily have been rewritten.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; stripping out penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opponent creates against you; measures true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall quality.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — Senegal's Matchday-1 chance quality was decent, yet finishing failed.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value the more aggressive the press (Senegal low value).
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than raw possession.

  • Senegal's signature is African-champion-level physical duels + wide pace + a high press — the foundation of AFCON 2021 champions and World Cup 2022 R16 is there. Against France in the opener their creativity was on point, but the finishing failed.
  • Cross-reference: World Cup 2022 R16 (lost to England), AFCON 2021 title (won on penalties vs Egypt) — Senegal's floor is not low, but a toothless attack is a recurring concern (Jackson/Sarr's conversion).
  • This match's approach: a do-or-die game forces them to commit forward fully, leveraging their physicality and pace and improving finishing efficiency; but they must beware being caught in behind by Haaland/Nusa's counters once they push up — this is the thin line between Senegal turning it around and crashing out.
