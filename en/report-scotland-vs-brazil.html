📋 Quick Summary (Read This First)

This is a Group C final round, an "asymmetric motivation" duel: Brazil on 4 points (1-1 draw vs Morocco + 3-0 win vs Haiti, GD +3) are all but qualified, the goal being to fight for top spot; Scotland on 3 points (1-0 win vs Haiti + 0-1 loss vs Morocco, GD 0) must beat Brazil for any qualification chance, and still need to watch the simultaneous Morocco-Haiti result. The historical dimension is deeply unfriendly to Scotland — Scotland have never beaten Brazil at a major tournament, and Brazil have never historically lost to Scotland (David Narey's astonishing "toe-poke" long shot in 1982 still ended in a 4-1 Brazil win), and Scotland have never reached a group exit in 12 World Cups/Euros. Three talking points: ① Raphinha's absence + possible Brazil rotation and the impact on their ceiling for breaking a packed defence; ② can Scotland's deep block + counters + set pieces produce an upset in a last stand; ③ the motivation gap — Brazil conservative vs Scotland all-out. ③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; stripping out penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opponent creates against you; measures true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall quality.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = many long-range efforts / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
