① Score progression

Portugal delivered a composed, emphatic rout in Houston, sealing the win 3-0 by half time. 6' Ronaldo opened the scoring, 17' Nuno Mendes extended it with a long-range strike, and 39' Ronaldo's brace made it 3-0 at the break. In the second half, 60' Uzbekistan goalkeeper Nematov turned the ball into his own net for 4-0, and 87' Rafael Leão came off the bench to seal 5-0. Ronaldo became the first player ever to score at six different World Cups, and with his 10th World Cup goal overall overtook Eusébio to become Portugal's all-time World Cup top scorer.

③ Tactical review

Ronaldo's record night: six straight World Cups scoring + Portugal's all-time top
Ronaldo scored a brace, becoming the first player ever to score at six consecutive World Cups, and overtook Eusébio as Portugal's all-time World Cup top scorer (10 goals). Transferable conclusion: even as he ages, Ronaldo's finishing instinct in the box remains a stable source of Portugal goals; against stronger opponents in the final round they must still protect his shooting space.
Nuno Mendes on the left: a two-way match best (Sofascore 8.8)
Mendes not only scored from distance, his overlapping runs and recovery work on the left were both top-tier. Transferable conclusion: Portugal's attacking width relies heavily on both full-backs overlapping, and Mendes on the left is the most reliable second attacking outlet.
2.41 xG for 5 goals — a complete domination with both efficiency and chance volume
Portugal were not a "few chances, many goals" lucky run; they genuinely created plenty of quality chances and converted them cleanly. Transferable conclusion: Portugal's attacking firepower has been validated, and they retain a high ceiling for the knockouts.
Uzbekistan 0.25 xG, just 2 shots — attack completely silenced
The World Cup newcomers lacked means to transition from defence to attack against a European power. Transferable conclusion: Uzbekistan need to rediscover their organisation against a more even opponent in the final round, otherwise a win will be hard to come by.

③ Deep-metric quick reference (what these "xG-like" metrics each represent)

xG / npxG: the total quality of shooting chances; stripping out penalties better reflects open-play creativity.
xGA / xGC: the quality of chances the opponent creates against you; measures true defensive level.
xGD / 90: xG−xGA, the single best proxy for overall quality.
xG per shot: the quality of each shot — a low value = many long-range efforts / poor chances.
PPDA: passes allowed per defensive action; the lower the value the more aggressive the press.
Field tilt: share of final-third touches; measures territorial tilt rather than raw possession.
