This is the Round of 32's "extreme mismatch, David-vs-Goliath" tie — reigning champions Argentina topped Group J with a perfect record (9 pts, 8 scored, 1 conceded), while World Cup debutants Cape Verde made history by reaching the knockouts unbeaten from Group H via three draws (including a stunning 0-0 hold of Spain). The winner advances straight to the Round of 16; a draw goes to extra time / penalties. Odds and supercomputers agree emphatically: Argentina are overwhelming favorites. Advance market: Argentina to advance -600, Cape Verde to advance +1400. The biggest talking points: ① Messi returns to the XI — rested last round, he already has a tournament-leading 6 goals (a men's World Cup all-time scoring record) and partners Lautaro up top (Julián Álvarez drops out); ② can Cape Verde reproduce the deep, compact block + Vozinha heroics that held Spain and drag the game to a low score or even extra time; ③ will Argentina get complacent / when do they break the deadlock. Base case: Argentina control via individual quality and overall strength, chasing a multi-goal win to advance; Cape Verde bank on a low block + Vozinha + sporadic counters (Ryan Mendes / Livramento) to nick something or run down the clock.
